SURGE OF YOLANDA

The surge of Typhoon Yolanda (Haiyan) was a devastating event, with towering waves up to 20 feet crashing into coastal areas, particularly in Eastern Visayas. The intense flooding overwhelmed cities like Tacloban, wiping out homes, infrastructure, and entire communities. The destruction left many residents trapped and made rescue efforts incredibly difficult. In the aftermath, the surge contributed to a widespread humanitarian crisis, displacing millions and leaving them without basic needs like food, water, and shelter. The global response to the disaster highlighted the urgent need for improved disaster preparedness, especially in vulnerable coastal regions. The catastrophic event underscored the increasing risks posed by climate change and extreme weather.
